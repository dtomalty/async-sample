What is async? ↓
Fewer, better meetings
Unnecessary meetings waste an estimated $37 billion in salary hours a year in the U.S. alone, tanking both worker productivity and job satisfaction.

Async work replaces unproductive, morale-killing meetings with writing or prerecorded video, so everyone can catch up on their own schedules.

A framework for identifying which meetings to take async →
Not expecting an immediate response
Fast responses may feel productive, but they require everyone to work in a constant state of reactive context-switching making it impossible to focus.

Async work allows people to create long, uninterrupted blocks of time for deep work and chunk their communication time together instead of spreading it throughout the day.

Why context-switching kills your team's productivity (and what to do about it) →
Tracking outcomes, not hours
Measuring performance by hours worked keeps people chained to the traditional workday and incentivizes presenteeism rather than meaningful productivity.

Async work lets people choose the hours that work best for them and puts the focus back where it should be: on the actual work getting done.

3 time-tested systems for building outcome-based accountability →
Focusing deeply, then disconnecting completely
Back-to-back meetings and hyper-responsiveness leave people feeling exhausted with little to show for it. The urgent gets prioritized over the important.

Async work gives people the time and mental space to make progress on meaningful work during the workday, then disconnect to recharge and be fully present in their lives.
